## Project Overview

This project implements **K-Means Clustering**, an unsupervised machine learning algorithm, to segment customers based on their **Annual Income** and **Spending Score**.

The project includes an interactive **Streamlit Dashboard** that allows users to explore customer data, visualize patterns, analyze customer segments, and predict the cluster of new customers based on their income and spending behavior.

## Dataset

The project uses the **Mall Customers Dataset**, which contains customer information including:

* Customer ID
* Gender
* Age
* Annual Income (k$)
* Spending Score (1–100)

The dataset is analyzed to identify different customer groups based on purchasing behavior.

## Machine Learning Approach

### K-Means Clustering

K-Means groups customers into clusters by finding similarities between customers based on:

* Annual Income
* Spending Score

The optimal number of clusters was identified using the **Elbow Method**, and the model successfully divided customers into **five distinct segments**.

---

## Results

The model successfully created five customer segments:

* **High Income & High Spending Customers** – Potential premium customers
* **High Income & Low Spending Customers** – Customers requiring engagement strategies
* **Low Income & High Spending Customers** – Frequent buyers with lower income
* **Low Income & Low Spending Customers** – Low-value customers
* **Average Customers** – Medium income and medium spending behavior

These insights can help businesses improve marketing strategies and customer targeting.
